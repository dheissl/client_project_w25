UMSI Student Resources

Academic Support Services

At UMSI, we offer a variety of resources to ensure academic success. Whether you need help with coursework, writing, or research, these services can guide you every step of the way:

  • Tutoring Services: Access tutoring for various subjects through the UMSI Tutoring Website.
  • Python Learning: Enhance your coding skills with online Python resources.
  • Writing Assistance: The Sweetland Writing Center provides support for writing assignments, from brainstorming ideas to polishing drafts.
  • English Language Improvement: The English Language Institute (ELI) offers support for non-native English speakers.
  • University Library Resources: Explore a wealth of digital and physical materials available at the University Library.
  • Laptop Specifications: Check out the recommended student laptop specifications for optimal academic performance.
  • Support for Disabilities: Services for Students with Disabilities (SSD) ensure equal access to academic resources.

Health and Wellness Resources

Taking care of your mental and physical health is crucial for your academic success. These resources are available to support your well-being:

  • Embedded CAPS Counselor: UMSI has an embedded counselor through Counseling and Psychological Services (CAPS) to assist students with mental health needs.
  • University Health Services (UHS): Comprehensive health services are available through UHS, including counseling and medical care.
  • Peer Counseling: Students can engage with peers trained in counseling for support and guidance.
  • Food Security: The Maize & Blue Cupboard provides food access for students in need.
  • Interactive Mental Health Support: Utilize the SilverCloud platform for interactive mental health resources.
  • Wolverine Support Network: Connect with a peer support network for assistance and community building.
  • Wolverine Wellness: A range of wellness programs to help students maintain a healthy balance.

Building Community and Finding Support

Engage with these campus organizations to connect with fellow students and find support:

  • UMSI Student Life: Get involved in student life events and activities that foster community connections.
  • First-Generation Student Center: Specialized resources and support for first-generation college students.
  • MESA (Multi-Ethnic Student Affairs): Programs for students of color to create a sense of belonging and support at U-M.
  • Spectrum Center: Dedicated resources for LGBTQIA2S+ students, including community building and advocacy.

Additional Helpful Resources

In addition to academic and wellness support, the following resources can assist with various needs:

  • CEW+ (Center for the Education of Women+): Provides support for women and underserved individuals at U-M.
  • UM Dean of Students: Offers assistance with personal challenges that may affect your academic performance.
  • International Center: Provides guidance and resources for international students.
  • Student Caregivers: Access resources for students balancing academics with caregiving responsibilities.

Emergency and Supportive Contacts

In case of emergency or urgent support, here are important contacts to keep in mind:

  • Emergency Services: Call 911 for immediate help in emergencies.
  • Non-Emergency Safety: Contact the University of Michigan Division of Public Safety at 734-764-1131 for non-urgent safety concerns.
  • 24/7 Mental Health Support: Call 734-764-8312 for urgent mental health assistance through CAPS.
  • Sexual Assault Support: The Sexual Assault Prevention and Awareness Center offers support for survivors. Call their 24/7 hotline at 734-936-3333.
  • SafeRide Late Night Transportation For safe rides within 1 mile of campus, call 734-647-8000 (available to students with MCard).

Other Questions

If you need help navigating these resources or have questions, reach out to the MSI Academic Advising team at umsi.advising.msi@umich.edu or UMSI Academic Success at umsi.academicsuccess@umich.edu.
