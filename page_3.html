Counseling and Psychological Services (CAPS)

Introduction

CAPS is dedicated to supporting the mental health and well-being of University of Michigan students. We offer a variety of counseling services, including in-person and virtual sessions, to assist students in managing the stresses of university life, developing coping strategies, and promoting overall mental wellness.

Our services are inclusive, multicultural, and guided by a multidisciplinary approach. We strive to ensure that every student has access to the resources they need to maintain their mental health. You can visit our front desk at Michigan Union (4th Floor) or reach us by phone at (734) 764-8312 for assistance and support.

Scope of Services

  • Clinical Services: CAPS offers a wide range of clinical, preventative, and training services to support students' mental health. Our licensed professionals provide individual, group, and relationship therapy in a culturally sensitive environment, addressing concerns such as anxiety, depression, stress, and academic performance.
  • Therapy Services: We provide confidential therapy for students to explore concerns, set goals, and develop coping skills. Our services include individual and group therapy, with specialized teams for issues like eating concerns and gender-affirming care.
  • Crisis Services: CAPS offers same-day access to a counselor for urgent issues, including safety concerns, trauma, or stress. We also provide 24/7 after-hours support and group crisis response for traumatic events.
  • Consultation: We offer consultation to faculty, staff, and others to help identify and address mental health concerns early, ensuring students get the support they need.
  • Embedded Therapists: Therapists are embedded in 18 academic units to provide tailored support, including direct counseling and faculty consultations, aligned with specific academic needs.
  • Care Management: Care management helps students connect with resources on and off-campus, offering support for complex situations like navigating insurance or finding affordable services.
  • Outreach and Engagement: We engage in campus-wide outreach to raise awareness about mental health, reduce stigma, and provide educational programming. This includes events, workshops, and peer support groups.
  • Training Programs: CAPS offers training programs to prepare mental health professionals with practical experience and education, meeting accreditation standards and focusing on empowerment and social justice.
  • Service Limitations: CAPS uses a short-term therapy model and may refer students for longer-term care or specialized treatment when needs exceed our scope, such as ongoing mental health issues or severe distress.

Support During Times of Crisis

CAPS acknowledges the emotional toll that current events, such as the violence in Israel and Gaza, may have on our students. We extend our support to those affected by this crisis and encourage self-compassion during difficult times. Additionally, we offer guidance and resources for coping with social injustices and bias incidents on campus.

Our team is here to support students navigating any emotional distress, and we offer confidential services to ensure that all students feel safe and supported in their mental health journeys.

Urgent and After-Hours Support

If you are in need of immediate mental health support, we offer urgent care services during office hours (Monday - Friday, 10:00 AM - 4:30 PM). After hours, 24/7 support is available through the CAPS crisis line.

Sexualized Violence and Support

We acknowledge that sexual violence affects individuals from all walks of life. CAPS is here to provide support for those impacted by sexual violence, ensuring that survivors receive compassionate care and resources to support their healing journey.

First-Year Guide

For first-year students, CAPS has created a resource called “Go Blue! A New Student's Guide to Surviving and Thriving at U-M.” This guide offers valuable mental health tips to help you transition smoothly into university life.

Other Services

CAPS provides several services to help students navigate their mental health journey:

  • Let's Talk - A free, informal program that offers students the chance to have a casual conversation with a counselor for initial support.
  • Peer Counseling (IPC) - A supportive space where students can talk to trained peers about their mental health concerns.
  • Teletherapy with Uwill - Free video, phone, and chat therapy sessions with licensed professionals, providing greater flexibility for students.
  • Togetherall - An online, anonymous peer-support platform for students to connect with others facing similar mental health challenges.
